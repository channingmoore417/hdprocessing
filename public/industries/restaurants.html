Restaurant Payment Systems That Work Against You

01

Slow Terminals Kill Table Turns

A busy Friday night on Jefferson Street is no place for a terminal that hangs on approval or a printer that jams twice a service. Slow payment systems cost you table turns — and in a Lafayette restaurant, table turns are revenue.

02

No Tableside Payment Option

Customers in Acadiana increasingly expect to pay at the table or counter. Running a card back to a terminal slows service, creates security exposure, and leaves your staff running laps they shouldn't have to run.

03

Tip Adjust Headaches

Full-service restaurants live and die by tip workflows. If your processor doesn't handle tip adjustment cleanly — or if your staff is adding tips manually at close — you're inviting errors, chargebacks, and a nightmare end-of-night reconciliation.

04

Waiting Days for Your Money

A Lafayette restaurant that processes $8,000 on a Saturday night shouldn't wait until Tuesday to see those funds. Food costs, staff wages, and vendor payments don't pause for your processor's settlement schedule.
